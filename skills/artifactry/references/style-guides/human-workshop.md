# Human Workshop

Human Workshop is a warm, readable style for teaching, internal enablement, workbooks, bootcamps, onboarding material, reflection exercises, and practical team documents. It inherits from warm workspace products, education-friendly editorial systems, conversational SaaS, and paper-led interface design in the local corpus.

## Core Idea

The artifact should feel like a thoughtful facilitator prepared it for humans who need to learn, discuss, and act. The style is calm, tactile, and structured. It should reduce anxiety, create pacing, and make every exercise feel doable.

## Visual Signature

- Warm paper canvas instead of sterile white.
- Soft earth, terracotta, muted green, and gentle blue accents.
- Serif or humanist display moments paired with a very readable sans body.
- Modular worksheet sections, reflection fields, small prompts, and margin notes.
- Low contrast decoration, visible reading rhythm, and clear activity blocks.

## Palette

Use cream, warm gray, soft brown ink, terracotta, sage, muted gold, and occasional blue. Accents should feel like editorial annotations or sticky notes, not product CTAs. Dark pages may be used for section breaks, but keep them warm brown/black rather than cold black.

## Typography

Headlines can use a warm serif or humanist sans. Body text must be highly legible for long reading. Use italic accents sparingly for reflective questions or key phrases. Mono is optional and should appear only for step numbers, time boxes, or exercise labels.

## Layout System

Use workbook pacing. Alternate explanation pages with exercise pages. Maintain generous margins and consistent section headers. Prefer two-column layouts for "idea + action", "example + your turn", and "before + after". Use large writing fields, checklists, and soft bordered panels.

## Slide Recipes

- Cover: warm title, short promise, 3 learning outcomes.
- Concept: one large idea, one explanation, one example box.
- Exercise: prompt, time limit, input fields, expected output.
- Discussion: question at top, 3 possible directions, facilitator note.
- Before/after: weak prompt vs strong prompt, old workflow vs AI workflow.
- Checklist: 5 to 7 steps with checkbox affordance.
- Reflection: "What changed?", "What will I try?", "What needs support?"
- Closer: recap, next action, resource pointer.

## DOCX Translation

DOCX should feel like a polished handout. Use readable headings, spacious paragraphs, shaded callouts, tables for exercises, and repeated worksheet fields. Keep page breaks intentional. Use warm accent rules, not heavy backgrounds.

## Agent Rules

- Convert abstract content into learnable chunks.
- Prefer plain language over dense consultant phrasing.
- Add exercise structure only when it helps the user's actual document.
- Keep Vietnamese diacritics large and comfortable.
- Do not compress too much content onto one slide.

## Avoid

Avoid cold enterprise dashboards, overly slick product gradients, tiny text, dense tables, aggressive black backgrounds, and decorative illustrations that distract from learning.
