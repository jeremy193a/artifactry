# Broadsheet Intelligence

Broadsheet Intelligence is an editorial research style for market analysis, strategy essays, policy explainers, industry maps, thought leadership, and serious social carousels. It inherits from newspaper systems, technology magazines, editorial AI pages, and research-led documentation.

## Core Idea

Make analysis feel published, not merely formatted. The artifact should have editorial judgment: hierarchy, pacing, sources, captions, sidebars, and strong thesis framing. It should reward close reading while still scanning well.

## Visual Signature

- Paper-white or near-cream canvas with black ink.
- Serif display headline, sans or serif body, mono metadata.
- Dense grid, hard rules, captions, source notes, and pull quotes.
- Occasional high-voltage accent for editorial emphasis.
- Footnotes and source labels treated as design elements.

## Palette

Use paper, ink, muted gray, one editorial blue/green/red/violet accent, and optionally a pale tint for charts or sidebars. Avoid glossy gradients. Dark pages can appear for dramatic chapter breaks but should feel like print ink, not app UI.

## Typography

Use high-contrast serif for headlines when available. Body must remain readable. Use mono or condensed sans for issue labels, page numbers, source names, and chart labels. Italic can carry emphasis, but do not overuse it.

## Layout System

Use newspaper logic: columns, gutters, section labels, rules, captions, sidebars. Strong pages often combine one large thesis headline with a structured evidence block. Keep visual density high but controlled.

## Slide Recipes

- Cover: issue label, thesis headline, subtitle, source strip.
- Question: one provocative question plus evidence answer.
- Big number: massive value with caption and source.
- Evidence grid: 2x2 or 3x2 cells, each with one claim.
- Timeline: dated editorial rows with short annotations.
- Quote: large pull quote, speaker/source, context note.
- Map/diagram: simplified vector evidence, caption below.
- Closer: editorial conclusion and what to watch next.

## DOCX Translation

DOCX should become a report or essay with strong headings, callout quotes, source notes, and tables. Use page headers like a publication section. Keep body reading comfortable.

## Agent Rules

- Preserve sources and caveats.
- Rewrite vague claims into thesis plus evidence.
- Use captions under charts and images.
- Do not make every slide a poster; vary between thesis, evidence, quote, and diagram.
- Validate small source text at mobile preview size.

## Avoid

Avoid generic SaaS cards, glossy UI chrome, fake newspaper texture, unsourced claims, and overly decorative magazine collage.
