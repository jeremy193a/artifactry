# Black Label Cinema

Black Label Cinema is a premium dramatic style for high-stakes pitches, portfolio presentations, luxury product stories, founder narratives, strategic reveals, and hero decks. It inherits from cinematic automotive sites, premium commerce, space/film imagery, and dark editorial launch systems.

## Core Idea

Make the artifact feel expensive by subtracting. The style is not dark mode; it is cinematic restraint. One phrase, one image, one metric, or one object can own the page.

## Visual Signature

- True black or warm near-black canvas.
- Large white or off-white display type.
- Scarce metallic, red, gold, or blue accent.
- Full-bleed imagery, if used, with deterministic text overlays.
- Vast negative space, monumental scale, and slow reveal pacing.
- Fine hairlines and small captions that feel like gallery labels.

## Palette

Use black, charcoal, white, muted silver, and one precious accent. Accent should appear like a signal: a rule, page mark, spec value, route line, or single word. Avoid colorful gradients unless the brief explicitly calls for aurora or performance energy.

## Typography

Use a refined sans or editorial display. Headlines can be huge and sparse. Avoid heavy bold unless it is part of a performance story. Metadata should be small, uppercase, and letter-spaced only enough to feel deliberate.

## Layout System

Use cinematic frames. Content can sit low, left, or center depending on the image. Split pages into title frame, evidence frame, object frame, and close frame. If there is no real image, create abstract black surfaces with rules, spec panels, and controlled light.

## Slide Recipes

- Cover: one massive title, one line of metadata.
- Hero object: image or silhouette, short caption, one accent line.
- Spec reveal: 3 to 5 values in a quiet strip.
- Contrast: black page with one white assertion.
- Narrative beat: short paragraph plus chapter marker.
- Portfolio grid: 3 images/cards with strict captions.
- Decision moment: one recommendation, one reason, one risk.
- Closer: final line and subtle footer.

## DOCX Translation

For DOCX, do not force black pages throughout. Use a premium cover, strong section dividers, black heading rules, and occasional dark callout pages if PDF output is expected.

## Agent Rules

- Cut copy aggressively.
- Let scale and spacing create emotion.
- Use imagery only when it adds specificity.
- Do not place text over busy image zones.
- Validate contrast and safe margins on every frame.

## Avoid

Avoid cheap luxury cliches, gold overload, random smoke/glow, crowded card grids, stock-looking dark photos, and motivational-poster layouts.
