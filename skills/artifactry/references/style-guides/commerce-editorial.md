# Commerce Editorial

Commerce Editorial is a polished product and brand explanation style for retail, catalogs, product launches, creator commerce, food/beverage, travel, and service offerings. It inherits from commerce platforms, lifestyle marketplaces, hospitality surfaces, warm product brands, and editorial retail storytelling.

## Core Idea

Make a product or offer feel desirable and understandable. The style combines commerce clarity with editorial warmth: image, benefit, detail, proof, action.

## Visual Signature

- Warm canvas, product tiles, caption systems, and confident CTAs.
- Product or offer appears in the first viewport/slide.
- Rounded but not childish cards.
- Soft bands, tags, price/detail rows, and trust markers.
- Alternation between hero product, benefits, proof, and action.

## Palette

Use warm white, cream, charcoal, muted green, coral, gold, or category-specific accent. Do not use too many retail colors at once. Let product imagery influence secondary tones.

## Typography

Use a friendly sans or a warm editorial pairing. Headlines should be direct and benefit-led. Labels and price/details can be compact and structured. Avoid overly decorative script fonts.

## Layout System

Use product grids, feature tiles, comparison cards, testimonial blocks, and detail strips. The reader should always know what is being offered, why it matters, and what to do next.

## Slide Recipes

- Cover: product/offer signal, title, short value prop.
- Product hero: image, 3 benefit tags, CTA/detail row.
- Feature breakdown: image or icon, benefit, proof.
- Catalog grid: products/services with consistent metadata.
- Testimonial/proof: quote, metric, customer/context.
- Comparison: basic vs premium, old vs new, manual vs assisted.
- FAQ: common objection with direct answer.
- Closer: offer summary and next step.

## DOCX Translation

DOCX should become a polished sales sheet or proposal. Use product tables, benefit callouts, comparison sections, and clear CTA/contact block.

## Agent Rules

- Put the product or offer early and clearly.
- Use images only if they help inspect the thing.
- Keep benefit copy concrete.
- Avoid generic lifestyle mood when the user needs details.
- Validate price/spec rows and CTAs for legibility.

## Avoid

Avoid vague brand fluff, dark stock imagery, excessive gradients, fake testimonials, and product cards with no real information.
