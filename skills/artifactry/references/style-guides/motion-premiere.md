# Motion Premiere

Motion Premiere is a cinematic creative-tech style for AI video, media launches, creator tools, campaign narratives, and dramatic product reveals. It inherits from film-festival editorial systems, creative AI platforms, dark media pages, waveform/audio tools, and launch trailer pacing.

## Core Idea

The artifact should feel like a sequence of frames. Each slide is a beat in a trailer: establish, reveal, prove, escalate, resolve.

## Visual Signature

- Dark hero frames mixed with paper-white reading frames.
- Frame marks, timecode labels, sequence numbers, waveform or timeline motifs.
- Large cinematic headlines with sparse body copy.
- Strong image/video still zones if assets exist.
- Accent used like a production marker, not a decorative color wash.

## Palette

Use black, charcoal, white, cool gray, and one accent: coral, electric blue, violet, or warm amber. Keep gradients subtle and atmospheric. Paper-white frames can carry detailed explanation between dark hero beats.

## Typography

Use a refined sans or film-editorial display. Timecodes and technical labels should use mono. Keep headlines short and dramatic. Body copy should feel like production notes.

## Layout System

Think in story beats. Use full-frame hero, split still/copy, timeline strip, before/after frames, storyboard grid, and closer title card. Do not make every slide the same card layout.

## Slide Recipes

- Title card: huge title, small timecode, sequence label.
- Establishing frame: image or abstract visual with one thesis.
- Cutaway: paper-white explanation frame with details.
- Timeline: scenes, steps, or model pipeline.
- Before/after: source input vs generated output.
- Storyboard: 4 to 6 frames with captions.
- Technical proof: model/tool stack as production credits.
- End card: final promise and output bundle.

## DOCX Translation

DOCX should become a treatment or creative brief. Use section title cards, storyboard tables, image captions, production notes, and timeline sections.

## Agent Rules

- Choose story beats before designing.
- Use assets when they strengthen the sequence.
- Keep text large and sparse on dark frames.
- Preserve detail on white explanatory frames.
- Validate all overlays on images.

## Avoid

Avoid generic cinematic smoke, fake film grain everywhere, random clapperboard icons, unreadable overlaid text, and one-note dark slides without pacing.
